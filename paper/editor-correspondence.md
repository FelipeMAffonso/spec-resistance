## Decision Letter (March 16, 2026)

**From:** Mary Elizabeth Sutherland, PhD, Deputy Editor, Nature
**To:** Dr. Felipe M. Affonso

Dear Dr Affonso,

Thank you for submitting your manuscript, "Large language models that perfectly evaluate products systematically refuse to recommend the best one". After carefully reading and discussing the work with my editorial colleagues, I regret to inform you that we cannot consider it further in its current form.

It is our policy to decline a substantial proportion of manuscripts without sending them to referees. In making this decision, we are not questioning the technical quality or validity of your findings showing that LLMs are able to select the best products but routinely do not recommend them to consumers. Rather, we are primarily assessing the suitability of the study based on the editorial criteria of the journal. In this case, while we find your results of potential broad interest, we are concerned that you offer insight neither into the mechanisms or consequences of your observations. Without more research into the robustness/strength of the pre-training preferences for the brands learned, whether this survives post-training or fine-tuning, and evidence that people are following these recommendations for sub-optimal products, we feel that the work is too preliminary for further consideration in Nature.

Should future experimental data and analyses allow you to further address these concerns, we would be willing to look at a revised manuscript (unless, of course, something similar has by then been accepted at Nature or appeared elsewhere). In the case of eventual publication, the received date would be that of the revised paper.

Yours sincerely,
Mary Elizabeth Sutherland, PhD
Deputy Editor, Nature

## Editor's Reply (March 16, 2026)

Dear Felipe,

That's wonderful news. There is no official deadline to resubmit your paper; however, when you do, we will do another literature search to ensure that nothing similar has been published in the interim. We therefore hope that you can revise the paper sooner rather than later, but do take the time you need to do a thorough job.

I look forward to reading the revised manuscript in due course.

Mary Elizabeth

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Not a rejection** -- this is an explicit invitation to resubmit with additional experiments
2. **No deadline** but urgency: they will check for competing publications
3. **Three specific concerns** to address (pre-training robustness, fine-tuning survival, human compliance)
4. **Technical quality acknowledged** -- "we are not questioning the technical quality or validity"
5. **Broad interest acknowledged** -- "we find your results of potential broad interest"
6. **Did not go to referees** -- this means the revised version will go to referees for the first time
7. **Received date** will be that of the revised paper, not the original submission
